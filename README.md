# Nano

NANO is a VR adventure prototype developed in Unity for Pico 4, where players control a nanobot inside the human body to identify and eliminate dangerous viruses. 
<br> Using scanning tools, crafted markers and environmental interactions, players assist the immune system by guiding T-cells toward infected targets while avoiding incorrect classifications that can destroy allied cells.

## Key Features
- Quest systems (involving scanning, crafting and virus-elimination objectives)
- Real-time health bars, distance indicators, kill counters, progress feedback and completion states
- Physics interaction systems
- VR gameplay prototyping

## Testing

Conducted multiple VR playtesting sessions to evaluate usability, player comfort and gameplay clarity on Pico 4.

**Key findings from testing:**

- Identified motion sickness and disorientation issues caused by vertical movement controls and zero-gravity navigation.
- Detected inconsistent scanner feedback and unclear interaction responses during virus scanning tasks.
- Observed that some players struggled to understand objectives without onboarding, leading to improvements in UI guidance and quest communication.
- Gathered feedback on environmental readability and interaction flow inside the blood vessel and nano ship environments.

**Actions taken based on feedback:**

- Refined scanner feedback systems and interaction responsiveness.
- Improved quest update popups and gameplay guidance to make objectives more intuitive.
- Iterated on movement behaviour to reduce dizziness and improve player comfort in VR.
- Adjusted environmental interaction cues to improve player orientation in zero-gravity gameplay sections.

**Results:**

- Reduced reported motion discomfort during testing iterations.
- Improved player understanding of gameplay objectives and virus identification mechanics.
- Increased interaction clarity through better UI feedback and scanner communication.
- Validated the educational concept by confirming that players understood the relationship between nanobots, viruses, and immune system responses after gameplay.
